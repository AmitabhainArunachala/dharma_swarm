# DGC Revenue Swarm Mission

Date: 2026-03-13
Purpose: run a 5-10 agent commercial mission that turns DGC's current capability into offers, pricing, proof, and target accounts
Primary reference: `docs/reports/DGC_SHAKTI_REVENUE_ARCHITECTURE_2026-03-13.md`

## Mission Thesis

Do not ask the swarm to "figure out the business."

Ask it to produce a bounded `Revenue Packet` that can directly support:

- outreach
- proposals
- design-partner conversations
- pricing decisions
- demo preparation

The mission is complete only when DGC has commercial artifacts that a human operator could use this week.

## Desired Output

The swarm should produce all of the following:

1. `icp_ranking.md`
   Top buyer segments ranked by pain, budget, speed, and DGC fit.
2. `offer_stack.md`
   Three to four concrete offers with promise, scope, exclusions, and delivery shape.
3. `pricing_sheet.md`
   Fixed-fee and retainer pricing with logic.
4. `proof_packet.md`
   Existing DGC artifacts and runtime evidence that support the sales story.
5. `demo_script.md`
   One short, high-conviction live demo narrative.
6. `target_accounts.md`
   25-50 design-partner candidates.
7. `outreach_angles.md`
   Short outbound narratives by ICP.
8. `success_metrics.md`
   What must improve for the buyer to renew, expand, or give a testimonial.
9. `risk_register.md`
   Delivery risks, product gaps, and claim boundaries.
10. `director_summary.md`
   One-page synthesis for immediate human use.

## Hard Rules

- No generic "AI transformation" language.
- No claim without an internal artifact, runtime proof, or official external source.
- No pricing without a delivery boundary.
- No ICP ranking without pain and budget logic.
- No demo that requires more than one primary narrative thread.
- No outreach copy that hides what is still brittle.
- No agent may invent customer names or design partners.

## Swarm Shape

Minimum viable swarm: `5 agents`
Full commercial swarm: `10 agents`

## Required Roles

1. `Director`
   Owns sequence, synthesis, and final packet quality.
2. `Buyer Mapper`
   Finds and ranks buyer types and target accounts.
3. `Pain Cartographer`
   Identifies operational pain and economic stakes.
4. `Competitive Lattice Analyst`
   Distinguishes DGC from adjacent products and substitutes.
5. `Proof Miner`
   Extracts supporting artifacts from the repo and existing runs.
6. `Offer Architect`
   Designs sharp commercial packages.
7. `Pricing Strategist`
   Designs fixed-fee, deployment, and continuation economics.
8. `Demo Director`
   Produces the shortest persuasive walk-through.
9. `Validator`
   Red-teams claims, proof, and brittleness.
10. `Closer`
   Converts outputs into concrete outreach and proposal assets.

## Dependency Graph

The swarm should work in this order:

### Wave 1: Market and pain discovery

- Buyer Mapper

- Competitive Lattice Analyst

Deliverables:
- ICP shortlist

### Wave 2: Proof and packaging

- Proof Miner

- proof packet

### Wave 3: Conversion assets

- Demo Director
- Closer
- Validator

Deliverables:

- outreach angles
- risk register

### Wave 4: Director synthesis

- Director

Deliverables:
- director summary
- go/no-go recommendation
- top next three commercial actions

## Core Questions

Every agent should answer only the questions relevant to its role.

Shared core questions:

- Who hurts enough to pay now?
- What exactly do they buy?
- What proof do we already have?
- What is still too brittle to promise?
- What is the shortest path to one more paid engagement?

## Definitions Of Good Output

### Good ICP ranking

- segments are distinct
- pain is specific
- budget logic is explicit
- speed-to-close is discussed
- DGC fit is defensible

### Good offer design

- clear promise
- clear scope
- clear exclusion
- clear artifact outputs
- clear continuation path

### Good proof packet

- references real local artifacts
- ties artifacts to buyer pain
- distinguishes prototype proof from production proof

### Good demo

- starts with mess
- shows compression
- shows prioritization
- shows one real artifact
- ends with what changed

## Mission Loop

1. build the buyer and pain map
2. select the top wedge
3. package the wedge into one offer ladder
4. extract proof for that ladder
5. design the demo
6. red-team all claims
7. prepare outreach assets
8. produce the director summary

## Stop Conditions

The swarm must stop when:

- one credible top ICP is chosen
- the offer ladder is clear
- the demo is short enough to run live
- the proof packet is sufficient for outreach
- the next commercial step is obvious

The swarm must not continue endlessly polishing language after the packet is commercially usable.

## Failure Modes

This mission fails if:

- agents generate abstract business language without proof
- pricing floats without delivery constraints
- demo design assumes magic autonomy
- the output ignores DGC's current reliability limits
- there is no direct path from the packet to a paid conversation

## Recommended First Wedge

Unless strong contrary evidence emerges, default to:

`AI-native founder -> Mission X-Ray -> Mission Sprint -> Continuous Mission Desk`

Reason:

- sharp pain
- short sales cycle
- easy narrative
- high proof density
- strong continuation path

## First Human Actions After Packet Completion

1. choose the top 25 targets
2. prepare one live demo
3. send tailored outreach to the first 10
4. close one paid Mission X-Ray
5. convert it into a Mission Sprint

## Bottom Line

The commercial swarm is not for brainstorming.

It is for producing a packet that creates one more paying customer and one more cycle of reality-tested DGC hardening.
