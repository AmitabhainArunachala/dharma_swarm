Hello Anthropic Economic Futures team,

I am submitting the attached proposal, "Measuring Labor Market Outcomes of Corporate Carbon Investment: Welfare-Tons as a Joint Workforce-Environmental Impact Metric," for consideration in the Anthropic Economic Futures Research Awards Program.

The project is a six-month empirical study requesting $35,000. It asks whether corporate carbon spending can be measured and allocated using a welfare-economic metric that jointly captures verified carbon outcomes and workforce-transition outcomes. The planned outputs are a formal metric specification, a publishable empirical feasibility study, open replication code, and a policy brief for AI companies and standards bodies.

The proposal is motivated by a specific gap: AI companies are making increasingly explicit energy, infrastructure, and community-investment commitments, but there is still no rigorous framework for evaluating whether environmental mitigation spending also improves labor-market outcomes for workers at greatest transition risk.

I would be glad to provide any additional materials or answer follow-up questions.
