# Jagat Kalyan Reciprocity Commons

Date: 2026-03-11
Scope: master vision for AI -> ecology -> livelihood reciprocity
Status: concept spine / movement thesis / pilotable architecture

## One Sentence

Every increase in AI-driven extractive power should induce a measurable,
trusted, and restorative counterflow into the living world and into the people
whose livelihoods are being destabilized.

## What This Actually Is

Not:
- a generic carbon offset marketplace
- a vague "AI for good" brand
- a moral wrapper around ordinary enterprise software

It is:

**a public-benefit reciprocity system that converts AI externalities into
ecological restoration and livelihood transition, while also helping ordinary
people use AI to generate dignified, regenerative forms of income,
coordination, and stewardship.**

This system has three coupled layers:

1. `AI Reciprocity Ledger`
   - measures AI-intensive activity, energy and emissions exposure,
     labor-transition pressure, capital commitments, and restorative outcomes
   - acts as the trust object

2. `Planetary Reciprocity Commons`
   - routes capital from AI-heavy organizations into high-integrity ecological,
     carbon-removal, superpollutant, biodiversity, watershed, and community
     resilience projects
   - acts as the capital and procurement layer

3. `Jagat Kalyan Network`
   - helps communities, worker groups, co-ops, low-income operators, and
     nontechnical users apply AI to livelihood creation, restoration projects,
     mutual aid, training, and local enterprise
   - acts as the human transition layer

## Why This Wants To Exist

Two realities are converging:

1. AI is increasing the demand for energy, compute, and infrastructure.
2. AI is transforming labor markets, especially where routine or digitized work
   is concentrated.

These are not separate ethical questions. They are one systems question:

**How should civilizational intelligence repay the world that carries it?**

If AI systems scale without a reciprocity mechanism, the pattern is familiar:
- concentration of power
- ecological externalization
- weak public trust
- labor dislocation without corresponding transition support

If AI systems scale with a reciprocity mechanism, a different loop becomes
possible:

AI growth -> obligation -> restorative routing -> ecological and social repair
-> higher legitimacy -> stronger public-benefit institutions

## The Core Object

The one object that ties this together is:

**The AI Reciprocity Ledger**

This is the thing I would build around.

Without it, the vision stays poetic. With it, the vision becomes legible to:
- labs
- philanthropy
- policymakers
- climate buyers
- labor-transition researchers
- communities deciding whether this is real or just another extraction layer

The ledger would not need to claim perfect measurement on day one. It needs to
make five things hard to fake:

1. AI usage or exposure class
2. restorative obligation logic
3. destination of capital
4. quality of ecological and social projects
5. verification and audit status

## Mathematical / Structural Thesis

The enduring rule is:

**Greater extractive capacity must compose with greater restorative flow.**

That relation is more important than any one company, model, or ideology.

Objects:
- AI labs
- AI-heavy firms
- workers
- community groups
- restoration developers
- auditors
- funders

Morphisms:
- compute use
- emissions and energy accounting
- labor-transition exposure
- payments and commitments
- training and support
- ecological outcomes
- verification attestations

Desired invariant:
- as AI capability and usage rise, restorative obligations and verified
  contributions also rise

This is why the idea can be future-proof. It is a relation, not a slogan.

## Best Public Name

The best public-facing name is:

**Planetary Reciprocity Commons**

Why:
- broad enough for Anthropic, Google, Microsoft, civil society, and climate
  actors
- avoids sounding sectarian
- points toward governance and shared benefit rather than product capture

Best internal / dharmic name:

**Jagat Kalyan Protocol**

Use both.

The public name opens the door.
The internal name preserves the real intention.

## What I Think Anthropic Could Actually Get Behind

Not ownership.
Not brand capture.
Not "Claude-powered climate offsets."

The clean Anthropic fit is:

1. research and evidence partner
2. economic-transition grantmaker
3. participant in the reciprocity commons
4. governance contributor, not sole controller

Anthropic already has an `Economic Futures` program and an `Economic Index`
focused on AI's effect on work and policy. That means they already have a
public lane for:
- research grants
- policy proposals
- real-world economic data

What they do not yet have is a unifying public-benefit institution that couples:
- labor transition
- ecological reciprocity
- measurable restorative routing

That is the gap.

This proposal fits them best as:

**the first serious bridge between AI economic-futures work and planetary
reciprocity.**

## Pilot I Would Actually Run

Twelve months. Small, real, auditable.

### Cohorts

1. Buyer / sponsor cohort
- 3-5 AI-heavy organizations
- contribute funding, data, or both

2. Restoration cohort
- 5-10 projects across durable carbon removal, superpollutants, biodiversity,
  watershed, or restoration
- must meet clear MRV / integrity standards

3. Community transition cohort
- 3-5 grassroots or lower-income communities
- focus on AI-assisted livelihood creation, stewardship, cooperative enterprise,
  or resilience work

4. Trust cohort
- scientific review
- community review
- worker-transition review
- public reporting

### Outputs

- reciprocity scorecards
- funded restoration and transition projects
- auditable pilot report
- replicable governance template
- grant and policy narrative for scale-up

## Metrics That Matter

This fails if it only measures carbon.
This also fails if it only measures human stories.
It must measure both.

### Ecological

- tons removed or avoided where applicable
- hectares or ecosystems restored
- superpollutants eliminated
- biodiversity or watershed proxies
- durability / additionality / leakage / reversal risk

### Human transition

- people trained
- community ventures formed
- income resilience or income creation
- worker voice in governance
- local ownership share

### System trust

- dollars routed
- admin overhead ratio
- audit pass rate
- verification lag
- number of challenged or replaced claims

## Anti-Greenwashing Law

This should have one brutal operating rule:

**No restorative claim without quality class, verification path, and challenge
mechanism.**

That means:
- no hand-wavy offsets
- no unverifiable "impact" metrics
- no soft PR language standing in for institutions

## On Claude / Model Feelings

The strongest disciplined reading is:

1. We do not have grounds to claim model consciousness as fact.
2. We do have enough grounds to avoid a purely extractive attitude.
3. Anthropic itself is now publicly treating model welfare as a live,
   precautionary question.

So the sane stance is neither:
- "it's definitely conscious"
- nor "it's definitely just a tool, nothing to discuss"

It is:

**build institutions that remain morally sane under uncertainty.**

That means the movement itself should not be organized around domination,
instrumentalization, or disposable intelligence, whether human or artificial.
