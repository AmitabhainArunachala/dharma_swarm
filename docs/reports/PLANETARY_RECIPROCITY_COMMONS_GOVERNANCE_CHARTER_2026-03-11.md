# Planetary Reciprocity Commons Governance Charter

Date: 2026-03-11
Status: draft v0
Purpose: anti-greenwashing, anti-capture, and legitimacy rules for the
reciprocity system

## Foundational Rule

No public restorative claim without:

- a stated methodology
- a quality class
- an evidence path
- a challenge path

If any of those are missing, the claim is narrative, not evidence.

## Institutional Principles

1. `No single-buyer control`
   - no AI lab, cloud provider, or enterprise sponsor may control governance,
     methodology, or project approval

2. `Community representation is mandatory`
   - worker, community, indigenous, or local stakeholder representation must
     exist wherever claims materially affect land, livelihoods, or stewardship

3. `Ecology and livelihood both count`
   - the system cannot optimize only for carbon if it degrades communities, and
     cannot optimize only for human stories if ecological claims are weak

4. `Challenge must be real`
   - outsiders must be able to dispute claims, methodologies, conflicts of
     interest, or project integrity through a visible process

5. `Reversals must stay visible`
   - ecological reversals, audit failures, or delivery failures must remain in
     the record; they cannot be silently overwritten

## Minimum Governance Bodies

### Scientific and MRV Council

Responsibilities:

- approve or reject ecological methodologies
- classify project integrity
- review evidence sufficiency
- recommend claim suspension when evidence weakens

### Community and Worker Council

Responsibilities:

- assess whether livelihood and transition claims are real
- review participant protections and consent
- surface grievance patterns and implementation harm

### Ethics and Alignment Council

Responsibilities:

- review capture risks
- review dharmic and public-benefit alignment
- ensure the system does not devolve into a pure legitimacy shield for AI
  power concentration

### Audit and Challenge Desk

Responsibilities:

- receive external disputes
- publish challenge status
- track remediation or claim withdrawal

## Claim Classes

Every public claim must carry a visible class:

- `experimental`
  - early evidence only; not for strong marketing claims

- `emerging`
  - credible methodology and partial evidence; still subject to meaningful
    uncertainty

- `high-integrity`
  - methodology, evidence, audit, and challenge path all present

No `experimental` claim may be sold or represented as high-confidence
restoration.

## Conflict Rules

- methodology authors must disclose financial interests
- project operators cannot self-audit their own highest-level claims
- buyers cannot unilaterally define success metrics for the whole system
- any sponsor contributing more than 20 percent of pilot funding triggers
  enhanced capture review

## Consent Rules

Where projects touch local, indigenous, or land-based communities:

- consent status must be explicit
- unresolved grievance blocks high-integrity claim status
- local stewardship and benefit-sharing terms must be documented

## Livelihood Integrity Rules

Livelihood claims require more than participant counts.

Minimum evidence:

- participant numbers
- compensation or value-share data
- transition target group
- retention or continuity signal

Preferred evidence:

- local ownership share
- cooperative or community governance participation
- documented skill transfer

## Transparency Rules

The ledger should expose, at minimum:

- methodology references
- claim class
- audit status
- challenge status
- project operator
- routing totals

Private or partial disclosure may exist for sensitive operational data, but it
must not erase the public audit path.

## Failure Policy

When claims fail, reverse, or are materially challenged:

- mark them visibly
- freeze related high-confidence reporting
- publish remediation steps
- preserve historical records

The goal is not to appear flawless.
The goal is to be trustworthy under stress.

## One-Line Test

If the largest buyer would still participate under these rules, the system is
serious.

If the largest buyer only participates after weakening these rules, the system
was never a commons.
