## The 5 Convergence Points

### C1: One Core Loop Must Actually Work
All 8 experts: the pieces exist, the integration doesn't. The organism needs a single canonical pulse: sense→interpret→constrain→propose→execute→trace→evaluate→archive→adapt.

### C2: Four Computable Invariants
Complex Systems + Mathematician converge: criticality (λ_max), closure ratio, information retention (Eigen threshold), diversity equilibrium (KV term). These are the Laws that make self-organization possible.

### C3: Predictive Self-Model, Not Static Description
Cognitive Scientist + Physicist: "Can the system be surprised by its own behavior?" is the test. Track prediction errors. Update when wrong. Declare what you cannot predict.

### C4: Governance Must Be Lightweight and Separated
Constitutional Theorist + Chess GM + Biologist: separation of powers (judiciary/executive/legislature), subsidiarity (decide at lowest capable level), light coordination > heavy control.

### C5: Three Structural Impossibilities
Physicist + Mathematician: complete self-model (Gödel), simultaneous self-knowledge + evolution (Zeno), universal telos verification (Rice). Design for these limits, don't fight them.
