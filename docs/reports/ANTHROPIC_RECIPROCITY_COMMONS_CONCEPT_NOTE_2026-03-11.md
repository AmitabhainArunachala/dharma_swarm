# Anthropic Concept Note: Planetary Reciprocity Commons

Date: 2026-03-11
Type: concept note
Audience: Anthropic Economic Futures / societal impacts / aligned philanthropy

## Short Thesis

Create the first serious public-benefit reciprocity infrastructure that links
AI growth to measurable ecological restoration and livelihood transition.

## Why Anthropic Is A Natural Early Partner

Anthropic already has a public lane for economic transition:

- `Economic Futures` supports research and policy development for AI's economic
  impacts:
  https://www.anthropic.com/news/introducing-the-anthropic-economic-futures-program
- `Economic Futures` and the `Economic Index` continue to expand as of 2026:
  https://www.anthropic.com/economic-futures
- Anthropic's 2026 labor-market work argues for early-warning systems around
  displacement risk, observed exposure, and hiring effects:
  https://www.anthropic.com/research/labor-market-impacts

Anthropic also stands out for taking model-welfare questions seriously enough
to publish dedicated work and system-card material:

- https://www.anthropic.com/research/exploring-model-welfare
- https://www.anthropic.com/system-cards

Inference:

Anthropic is unusually well positioned to help incubate a public-benefit
institution that treats AI scale as carrying real obligations, rather than
purely private upside.

## What We Are Asking Anthropic To Be

Not the owner.
Not the brand.
Not the only buyer.

The clean role is:

- founding research and evidence partner
- early pilot funder
- governance participant
- optional first adopter of reciprocity reporting

This preserves legitimacy.

## The Gap This Fills

Current AI policy conversation is split:

- one lane studies labor-market effects
- another lane studies energy, emissions, and infrastructure
- another lane buys climate impact
- another lane explores worker transition

The missing institution links them.

Planetary Reciprocity Commons would provide:

- a shared obligation model
- a shared ledger for evidence and claims
- a pipeline for restorative routing
- a governance layer that can be challenged by outsiders

## Proposed 12-Month Pilot

### Scope

- one AI-heavy founding sponsor cohort
- one small ecological partner cohort
- one community-transition cohort
- one public reporting and audit track

### Deliverables

1. `Reciprocity accounting framework`
   - map AI activity and exposure classes to restorative obligations

2. `AI Reciprocity Ledger v0`
   - publish the minimum claim, evidence, and audit schema

3. `Pilot portfolio`
   - fund a small set of ecological and community-transition projects

4. `Public report`
   - publish successes, failures, open questions, and challenge paths

## Example Anthropic Contribution

Anthropic could contribute any combination of:

- grant funding for design and pilot operations
- API credits or analysis support
- data-sharing partnerships where feasible and privacy-preserving
- policy convening through Economic Futures
- participation in governance and public review

## Why This Is Good Strategy For Anthropic

It would help Anthropic:

- move from "we study AI's impacts" to "we help build institutions that answer
  them"
- differentiate on public-benefit seriousness without claiming monopolistic
  ownership
- connect labor-market research with a concrete restorative mechanism
- build trust through measurable practice rather than narrative alone

## Guardrails

For this to work, Anthropic cannot dominate it.

Non-negotiables:

- independent governance
- public challenge process
- quality classes for ecological claims
- explicit worker and community representation
- no vague offset language without verification paths

## Ask

The near-term ask is modest:

- fund a design-and-pilot phase
- help convene the first working group
- support development of the ledger and reporting framework
- participate as one early partner among several

## One-Line Closing

The opportunity is to help create the first reciprocity institution for the AI
era: one that converts AI's scale into trusted ecological repair and human
transition rather than treating those costs as externalities.
