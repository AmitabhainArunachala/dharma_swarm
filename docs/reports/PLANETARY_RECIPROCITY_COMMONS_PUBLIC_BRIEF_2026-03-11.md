# Planetary Reciprocity Commons

Date: 2026-03-11
Type: public brief
Audience: labs, funders, climate actors, worker-transition groups, civil
society, aligned technologists

## One Sentence

Planetary Reciprocity Commons is a public-benefit initiative that turns AI's
ecological and labor externalities into verified ecological restoration and
livelihood transition.

## The Problem

AI is scaling on top of energy systems, data centers, supply chains, and labor
markets that absorb real costs.

Two pressures are becoming impossible to ignore:

- AI-driven infrastructure growth increases electricity demand and climate
  pressure
- AI-driven automation creates transition pressure across jobs and communities

These pressures are usually treated as separate issues. They are not.

They are both signs that intelligence is scaling without a reciprocity system.

## The Proposal

Planetary Reciprocity Commons creates that reciprocity system.

It connects four elements:

1. `AI usage and exposure`
   - compute, energy, emissions, and labor-transition pressure

2. `Obligation logic`
   - how AI-intensive activity maps to restorative commitments

3. `Restorative routing`
   - channeling funding and support into high-integrity ecological and
     community-transition work

4. `Verification and challenge`
   - public reporting, auditing, and challenge paths so claims cannot survive
     on branding alone

## The Core Object

At the center is an `AI Reciprocity Ledger`.

The ledger is not a blockchain pitch.
It is a trust object.

Its job is to make five things visible:

- what kind of AI activity occurred
- what restorative obligation followed from it
- where capital and support were routed
- what ecological and livelihood outcomes were claimed
- what evidence and verification status support those claims

## How It Works

On the ecological side:

- support high-integrity carbon removal, biodiversity, watershed, restoration,
  methane mitigation, and resilience projects
- prioritize quality class, verification path, and challenge process over PR

On the human side:

- support communities, worker groups, co-ops, and nontechnical operators using
  AI for dignified livelihood creation, local enterprise, training, stewardship,
  and mutual aid

The point is not just compensation.
It is regenerative capacity.

## Why Now

The case for a reciprocity layer is no longer hypothetical.

- Anthropic has an `Economic Futures` program focused on AI's economic impacts:
  https://www.anthropic.com/economic-futures
- Anthropic published new labor-market evidence on 2026-03-05 showing exposed
  occupations are projected to grow less and that hiring of younger workers may
  already be slowing in exposed occupations:
  https://www.anthropic.com/research/labor-market-impacts
- The IEA's 2025 `Energy and AI` work tracks the growing energy implications of
  AI and data centers:
  https://www.iea.org/reports/energy-and-ai
- The ILO's 2025 refined exposure index finds one in four workers globally are
  in occupations with some GenAI exposure:
  https://www.ilo.org/publications/generative-ai-and-jobs-refined-global-index-occupational-exposure

Inference from these sources:

The missing institution is not another lab study by itself.
The missing institution is a trusted mechanism that links AI scale to repair.

## What Makes This Different

This is not:

- generic ESG
- low-integrity offsetting
- a marketplace that treats communities as narrative props
- a single company CSR program

This is:

- measurable
- challengeable
- multi-stakeholder
- future-proofed around an invariant, not a slogan

The invariant is:

**greater AI-driven extractive capacity must induce greater verified restorative
flow**

## First Pilot

Start small and auditable:

- 3-5 AI-heavy sponsors
- 5-10 ecological project partners
- 3-5 community transition pilots
- independent scientific, community, and governance review

Deliverables:

- public reciprocity scorecards
- one auditable pilot report
- a governance charter
- a reusable ledger schema
