# Lodestones Index

`lodestones/` is a substrate layer, not a normative doctrine surface.

These files are meant to orient, provoke, reframe, and seed future work. They can be highly influential without being the final source of product, runtime, or repo truth.

## What A Lodestone Is

A lodestone document usually does one of four things:

- names or crystallizes an attractor
- reframes a design problem
- bridges conceptual worlds
- seeds a future line of inquiry or construction

That makes `lodestones/` different from:

- `foundations/`, which holds deeper conceptual canon and pillar-level substrate
- `docs/`, which carries current doctrine, plans, reports, and architecture
- `specs/`, which should hold normative engineering or protocol truth

## Local Topology

### Top-Level

- `CONSCIOUS_INFRASTRUCTURE.md`
  A high-signal orienting lodestone for the morphogenetic-field framing.

### `bridges/`

Cross-domain connectors that link one conceptual regime to another.

### `grounding/`

Research-grounded material that gives evidentiary footing to later reframes or seeds.

### `pillars_expanded/`

Expanded treatments of pillar material that sit adjacent to, but not identical with, the canonical substrate in `foundations/`.

### `reframes/`

Documents that explicitly replace an old frame with a stronger one.

### `seeds/`

Generative seed documents intended to spark future synthesis, architecture, or research directions.

## Handling Rule

Use lodestones to orient decisions, not to impersonate settled canon.

If a lodestone becomes:

- stable conceptual substrate, it may belong in `foundations/`
- active architecture doctrine, it may belong in `docs/architecture/`
- normative implementation truth, it may belong in `specs/`

Until then, keep it in `lodestones/` and treat it as a directional attractor.
