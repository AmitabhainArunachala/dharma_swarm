## 1. Carbon Market Sizing

### 1.1 Voluntary Carbon Market (VCM)

The voluntary carbon market exhibits wide variation in size estimates depending on methodology,
but all sources agree on strong growth momentum.

#### Current Market Size

| Source | 2024 | 2025 | 2026 (projected) |
|--------|------|------|------------------|
| InsightAce Analytic | -- | $1.6B | $1.7B |
| Roots Analysis | -- | ~EUR 2.5B | ~EUR 3.0B |
| Mordor Intelligence | -- | $15.83B | ~$23.8B |
| Allied Offsets | -- | -- | Growing |

The variation reflects definitional differences: narrow VCM (voluntary credit retirements only)
vs broad VCM (all carbon market activity outside compliance programs). The conservative estimate
($1.6-1.7B) counts only voluntary credit transactions. The Mordor Intelligence figure ($15.83B)
includes the broader carbon services ecosystem.

**Key signal**: In 2025, more than **USD 10 billion** was committed to new carbon credit
generation -- **3x the 2024 level**. Companies retired more credits in H1 2025 than in any
previous full year.

## 2. Ecological Restoration Economics

### 2.1 Cost Per Hectare by Ecosystem Type

#### Mangrove Restoration

| Metric | Value | Source |
|--------|-------|--------|
| Median implementation cost | **$8,143/ha** ($3,297/acre) | ScienceDirect 2025 |
| Alternative median | $2,099/ha | Mongabay 2025 |
| Global average | $9,739/ha | Busch 2025 |
| Indonesia | $3,900/ha | World Bank |
| Abandoned shrimp ponds (cheap) | $500-2,000/ha | Site-dependent |
| Eroded coastlines (expensive) | Up to $700,000/ha | Extreme cases |
| Opportunity cost | Can **double** total restoration expenses | Forgone aquaculture |

**Key insight**: Mangrove costs vary 1000x depending on site conditions. Rehabilitating
abandoned shrimp ponds is cheap; replanting eroded coastlines is expensive. Costs are lower
for larger projects, newer projects, in deltas, and in aquaculture ponds. Costs are higher
in richer countries, at eroded sites, and on open coasts.

Source: [ScienceDirect mangrove costs](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S259033222500168X),
[Mongabay](https://news.mongabay.com/short-article/2025/07/how-much-does-it-cost-to-restore-a-mangrove-forest/),

### 2.2 Carbon Sequestration Rates by Method

#### Sequestration Rates by Ecosystem

| Ecosystem | Annual Sequestration | Total Carbon Stock | Source |
|-----------|--------------------:|-------------------:|--------|
| **Mangroves** | 174 gC/m2/yr (1.74 tC/ha/yr) | **937 tC/ha** avg, up to 1,000 tC/ha | Donato et al., via Tandfonline |
| **Tropical forest** | 3-10 tCO2e/ha/yr (mature) | 150-250 tC/ha | IPCC |
| **Tropical reforestation** | 5-20 tCO2e/ha/yr (first 20 yrs) | Building to 150+ tC/ha | Chave et al. 2014 |
| **Grassland** | 0.01-7.08 tCO2/ha/yr | 0-50 tC/ha (soil) | IPCC, FAO |
| **Regenerative grassland** | 5-20 tCO2/ha/yr (high end) | -- | Regenerative ag claims |
| **Peatland** | 0.5-6 tCO2/ha/yr (rewetting) | 500-2,000 tC/ha (total stock) | IPCC Wetlands |
| **Seagrass** | 0.5-2 tCO2/ha/yr | -- | Unsworth et al. 2019 |

**Key insight for Jagat Kalyan**: Mangroves store **5-10x more carbon per hectare** than
tropical forests, primarily in sediments. The 937 tC/ha average stock makes mangroves the
highest-value ecosystem for carbon finance. Sediment accretion rate of ~5 mm/year ensures
ongoing sequestration.

Source: [Tandfonline mangrove carbon](https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/full/10.4155/cmt.12.20),
[Emission Index mangroves](https://www.emission-index.com/deforestation/mangrove-forest-carbon),
[Nature mangrove mapping](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41597-025-04881-5),
[FAO grasslands](https://www.fao.org/fileadmin/templates/agphome/documents/climate/AGPC_grassland_webversion_19.pdf),
[Science grassland SOC](https://www.science.org/doi/10.1126/science.abo2380)

## 4. Welfare-Ton Framework

### 4.1 The Formula

From `~/jagat_kalyan/WELFARE_TONS_SPEC.md` (v2.0):

$$W = C \\times E \\times A \\times B \\times V \\times P$$

| Factor | Name | Domain | What It Measures |
|--------|------|--------|-----------------|
| **C** | Verified CO2 tons | [0, inf) | Net tCO2e sequestered/avoided per year |
| **E** | Employment factor | [0, 5.0] | Job creation density x quality |
| **A** | Community agency | [0, 1.0] | Consent, ownership, self-determination |
| **B** | Biodiversity co-benefit | [0.8, 1.5] | Restoration type quality |
| **V** | Verification confidence | [0, 1.0] | Measurement trust level |
| **P** | Permanence factor | [0, 1.0] | Risk-adjusted durability |

**Critical design properties**:
- **Multiplicative**: Zero in ANY factor kills the entire product. No trading ecological
  harm for social benefit. No greenwashing.
- **Conservative defaults**: Missing data defaults to floor values. Rewards transparency.
- **Compatible with standards**: Carbon follows Verra VCS / Gold Standard methodology.
  Employment references ILO Decent Work. Biodiversity references Kunming-Montreal GBF.
  Verification compatible with ICVCM Core Carbon Principles.
- **Auditable**: Every welfare-ton decomposes into 6 factors, each into sub-indicators,
  each with cited source data.
