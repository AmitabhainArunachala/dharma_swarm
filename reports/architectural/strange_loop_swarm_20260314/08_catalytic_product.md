## Angle
Planner-level `F(S) = S` means the top-priority artifact set keeps regenerating the selector artifacts that choose it. Treat the strange loop as an artifact graph, not a cyclic step DAG: closure exists only when a strongly connected component contains discovery, planning, execution, and verification artifacts, and a completed run regenerates an upstream selector artifact. Prioritize by `closure_contribution * downstream_reuse * quality / max(duration_seconds, 1e-3)` first, then swap in token cost later.

## Blind Spots
- There is no artifact-native catalytic relation. `ConceptNode` and `ConceptEdge` represent concepts and static file relations, and `EdgeType` has no `CATALYZES` or `REGENERATES` relation in [semantic_gravity.py](/Users/dhyana/dharma_swarm/dharma_swarm/semantic_gravity.py#L40).
- `gravitational_mass()` and `snapshot()` measure density and hardening, not whether an artifact set reproduces more high-value artifacts or closes a selector loop in [semantic_gravity.py](/Users/dhyana/dharma_swarm/dharma_swarm/semantic_gravity.py#L500).
- `distill_notes()` and `extract_patterns()` surface findings and model/task leaders, but they do not preserve per-finding provenance, artifact IDs, or downstream reuse evidence in [distiller.py](/Users/dhyana/dharma_swarm/dharma_swarm/distiller.py#L87) and [distiller.py](/Users/dhyana/dharma_swarm/dharma_swarm/distiller.py#L218). I did not find direct unit tests for these heuristics.
- `WorkflowStep` and `WorkflowResult` store outputs and timing, but not `input_artifacts`, `output_artifacts`, `quality_score`, or reuse counts in [workflow.py](/Users/dhyana/dharma_swarm/dharma_swarm/workflow.py#L77). Also, `execution_order()` treats in-run cycles as errors, so strange-loop closure cannot be modeled as a cyclic DAG in [workflow.py](/Users/dhyana/dharma_swarm/dharma_swarm/workflow.py#L211).
- `related()` in [ecosystem_index.py](/Users/dhyana/dharma_swarm/dharma_swarm/ecosystem_index.py#L328) is term-overlap search, not catalytic evidence. It can suggest neighbors, but it cannot prove that one artifact caused another.
- Current ranking is readiness-oriented, not catalytic-yield-oriented: [semantic_briefs.py](/Users/dhyana/dharma_swarm/dharma_swarm/semantic_briefs.py#L81) optimizes for pass/score/mass, not closure contribution per unit cost.

## Risks
- If catalytic scoring is folded directly into FTS rank or raw gravitational mass, the planner becomes uninterpretable. Keep discovery, topology, and yield as separate signals.
- There is a real risk of confusing illegal DAG cycles with legitimate strange-loop closure. Preserve [workflow.py](/Users/dhyana/dharma_swarm/dharma_swarm/workflow.py#L211) as an acyclic executor and close the loop across persisted artifacts and reruns.
- Distiller heuristics can invent causal edges from prose. Require either workflow-consumption evidence or agreement between notes and evaluation history before counting an edge toward closure.
- Step metadata changes can disturb checkpoint compatibility around [workflow.py](/Users/dhyana/dharma_swarm/dharma_swarm/workflow.py#L354). Keep new fields additive during the paper window.
