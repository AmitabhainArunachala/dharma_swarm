# Campaign X-Ray Spec

Date: 2026-03-13
Offer stage: design partner
Status: ready to sell as a managed diagnostic

## Public promise

`Campaign X-Ray` takes a high-context technical workspace and returns the one campaign worth pursuing next, the bottlenecks blocking it, and the shortest verified path to a meaningful artifact.

## Ideal first buyer

- AI-native founder with too many repos, notes, branches, and active priorities
- small AI lab or technical studio drowning in experiments and documents
- founder office that needs continuity and decision compression more than another dashboard

## Inputs required

- one repo or workspace
- docs and notes
- current priorities or forcing question
- optional backlog / issue list
- one operator interview to define the current bottleneck

## Delivery packet

The buyer receives:

1. corpus structure map
2. top contradictions and bottlenecks
3. ranked semantic briefs
4. one selected campaign
5. one execution recommendation
6. one live walkthrough of the ledger continuity
7. one written next-step recommendation for a sprint

## Internal deliverable mapping

This internal packet maps those public outputs to concrete artifacts:

- semantic map:
  - `semantic_graph.json`
  - `semantic_proof.txt`
- ranked briefs:
  - `semantic_brief_packet.md`
- campaign continuity:
  - `campaign.json`
- proof and scope:
  - `proof_packet.md`
  - `risk_register.md`
- commercial close:
  - `pricing_sheet.md`
  - `outreach_pack.md`

## Exclusions

`Campaign X-Ray` does not promise:

- full autonomous execution
- production deployment
- exhaustive code implementation
- security / compliance review
- enterprise-grade guarantees
- access to hidden context the buyer does not provide

## Delivery sequence

### Day 0

- intake
- corpus handoff
- forcing question clarified

### Day 1

- digest corpus
- compile graph
- identify high-gravity clusters

### Day 2

- produce ranked brief packet
- select one campaign
- write contradiction and blocker map

### Day 3

- tighten proof boundaries
- prepare live walkthrough
- deliver packet and discuss sprint continuation

## Acceptance criteria

The X-Ray is successful if the buyer can answer all three questions at the end:

1. What is the one campaign that matters most right now?
2. What is blocking it?
3. What concrete artifact or sprint should happen next?

If the packet is interesting but does not clarify those three, it is not done.

## Continuation path

The conversion path is simple:

- `Campaign X-Ray`
  - diagnostic and prioritization
- `Campaign Sprint`
  - 1-2 week artifact delivery on the chosen campaign
- `Campaign Desk`
  - monthly continuation for continuity, steering, and proof

## Claim boundary

The honest claim is:

`DGC can compress a messy technical corpus into ranked campaigns and evidence-backed next steps.`

The dishonest claim would be:

`DGC can already autonomously run your company.`

Do not cross that line.
