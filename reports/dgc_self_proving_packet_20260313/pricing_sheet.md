# Pricing Sheet

Date: 2026-03-13
Status: recommended launch pricing for the first `Campaign X-Ray` design partners

## Recommended launch prices

| Offer | Scope | Timeline | Launch price | Standard price after proof |
|---|---|---|---:|---:|
| `Campaign X-Ray` | diagnostic, ranked briefs, selected campaign, live walkthrough | 3-5 business days | `$4,500` fixed | `$7,500-$12,000` |
| `Campaign Sprint` | one chosen campaign, one real artifact, verification packet | 1-2 weeks | `$15,000-$20,000` | `$20,000-$35,000` |
| `Campaign Desk` | monthly continuity, steering, briefs, and operator packet | 30 days | `$8,000/month` | `$12,000-$40,000/month` |

## Cold-start note

If there is no warm graph, prepend a lightweight `Signal Brief` to the stack.

This is:

- public-data-only
- used to earn reply and permission
- not the main revenue line
- a bridge into the paid `Campaign X-Ray`

## Why this launch pricing is right

- low enough to close quickly with a design partner
- high enough to force disciplined scope
- high enough that the buyer treats the work as serious
- still leaves room to raise after the first proof loop

## What the buyer gets at each tier

### Campaign X-Ray

- corpus intake
- semantic map
- contradiction and blocker scan
- ranked briefs
- one selected campaign
- one live walkthrough
- recommendation for sprint or no sprint

### Campaign Sprint

- everything from the X-Ray as context
- one bounded build or research campaign
- one artifact packet
- verification evidence
- one handoff or next-iteration memo

### Campaign Desk

- weekly priority packet
- live campaign steering
- continuity tracking
- artifact ledger
- monthly review and reroute

## Payment terms

Recommended:

- `Campaign X-Ray`
  - `50%` upfront
  - `50%` on delivery
- `Campaign Sprint`
  - `50%` upfront
  - `50%` on midpoint or delivery depending on scope
- `Campaign Desk`
  - monthly in advance

## Discounts

Use only one discount story:

`This is design-partner pricing in exchange for faster feedback and tighter operator collaboration.`

Do not say:

- `we are early`
- `this is beta so it is cheap`
- `we are still figuring it out`

## Scope protections

If the buyer wants any of the following, move them to a sprint or separate scope:

- code implementation beyond one bounded artifact
- production deployment
- security review
- staff training
- connector or integration work
- long-form research beyond the diagnostic packet

## Close logic

The offer stack should sell like this:

`Signal Brief -> X-Ray -> Sprint -> Desk`

Do not try to sell the desk cold.
